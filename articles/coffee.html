Quitting Coffee

I stopped drinking coffee at 9 AM and held off until 10 AM. Here’s what I learned about resilience, mindfulness, and becoming the person I always knew I could be.

At 9:01 AM, the withdrawal began.

My hands trembled slightly as I reached for my mug and remembered — it was empty. And it would remain that way for fifty-nine more unbearable minutes. I stared at the ceramic shell like it had betrayed me. But no. I had done this. I had made the choice to grow.

By 9:05, I started noticing the subtle details around me. The slight hum of the office lights. The breathing of my colleagues over the Slack huddle. My senses were sharpening. My mind, despite its gentle throb, was awakening to the present moment. This was mindfulness, I realized. Or early-onset caffeine deprivation psychosis. Either way: I was in it.

By 9:14, I had a profound realization about modern dependency. We are all just a few sips away from unraveling. Coffee isn’t a beverage; it’s a lifestyle crutch — a warm, bitter security blanket that keeps us comfortably numb. I was shedding mine. I was becoming someone… different. Someone stronger. Someone who listens to their body, and then sternly tells it “Not yet.”

At 9:32, I began journaling furiously. My thoughts were pure, primal, untethered by the jittery overconfidence of espresso. I wrote things like “Do bees know it’s Monday?” and “What if ambition is just dehydration?” I was unlocking truths — or at least Twitter drafts.

At 9:47, I lost feeling in my left eye. But I gained perspective.

And then, at 10:00 on the dot, I brewed a cup. Not because I needed it… but because I had earned it. The steam curled upward like a question mark. Who was I now? I was someone who had suffered. Who had waited. Who had looked the void of productivity in the face and said, “Not today. Give me fifty-nine minutes.”

I took a sip. It tasted like triumph.

Will I do it again? No. Absolutely not. That was hell.

But I’m not the same person I was at 8:59 AM. I’m better. I’m more patient. And above all, I’m wide awake — just as soon as the caffeine kicks in.

Namaste. ☕
