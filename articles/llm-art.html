LLM Art

We’re living in a golden age of automation and a garbage age of sincerity. Every time I blink, another algorithm is trying to replace something precious with a sanitized, scalable, engagement-optimized version of itself. And I don’t mean precious like “collectible,” I mean precious like waking up at 2 a.m. to scribble heartbreak on the back of a receipt.

I asked Gemini to write a villanelle about how poetry written by an AI isn't enjoyable.

Villanelle for the Algorithm’s Lament

A poem should breathe with more than patterned light, Not just cold echoes parsed from line to line. The algorithm crafts, but cannot write.

It captures grief in grayscale, never quite Reveals the mess beneath the measured spine. A poem should breathe with more than patterned light.

Its stanzas hum but never ache with fright, Its rhymes unfold in sequence too divine— The algorithm crafts, but cannot write.

It speaks of loss, but never dreams at night, Nor wakes with names it once had sworn were mine. A poem should breathe with more than patterned light.

Each verse is clean, too polished, too polite, No smear of ink, no rage, no flawed design. The algorithm crafts, but cannot write.

So I reject its song, however tight— Give me a verse that stumbles, breaks, and shines. A poem should breathe with more than patterned light. The algorithm crafts, but cannot write.

I admit I couldn't write a better one in a third of a second, though it's not exactly moving. But it's good enough that some people might think it is.
